# Content Scheduler

Posting consistently on social media is a full-time job — except you already have one. Content Scheduler lets you plan an entire week of posts in one sitting, then publishes them at the optimal times across Twitter/X, Instagram, LinkedIn, and more. It analyzes your audience engagement patterns to suggest when to post, helps you draft content that fits each platform's style, and makes sure you never go dark because you got busy.

## What It Does

- Schedules posts across multiple social platforms from a single queue with platform-specific formatting
- Analyzes your audience engagement data to suggest optimal posting times for maximum reach
- Generates post variations from a single idea — shorter for Twitter, professional for LinkedIn, visual for Instagram

## How It Works

1. Connect your social media accounts during setup (OAuth, no passwords stored).
2. Draft your posts or provide a topic and let the agent generate platform-tailored variations.
3. Review the weekly queue, adjust anything you want, and hit publish. Posts go out on schedule.

## Works With

- Twitter/X, Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook
- Threads, Bluesky
- Buffer (import/export)
- Google Sheets, Notion (content calendars)

## Example Usage

```
CONTENT QUEUE — Week of March 10-16

MONDAY
  8:00 AM  [LinkedIn] "3 things I learned from losing my biggest
           client last month..." (engagement post)
  12:15 PM [Twitter/X] Thread: Quick tips for freelance pricing
  6:00 PM  [Instagram] Behind-the-scenes photo of new product shoot

TUESDAY
  7:45 AM  [LinkedIn] Share blog post: "Why Small Teams Win"
  1:00 PM  [Twitter/X] Poll: "What's your biggest marketing challenge?"
  5:30 PM  [Instagram] Story: Customer testimonial graphic

WEDNESDAY
  9:00 AM  [Twitter/X] Industry news commentary
  12:00 PM [LinkedIn] Case study: Client results from Q4

ANALYTICS INSIGHT:
  Your LinkedIn posts at 7:45 AM get 3.2x more engagement than noon.
  Tuesday Twitter polls outperform other days by 58%.
  Recommendation: Move Thursday Instagram post to 6 PM (peak time).

QUEUE STATUS: 14 posts scheduled, 0 gaps this week
```
